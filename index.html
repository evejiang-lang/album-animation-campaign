Album animation campaign · three song moments

A quiet robot learns to be seen.

This microsite presents three short animations made as promotional visuals for different songs on one album. The campaign uses a house as an emotional container and a small robot as the album’s recurring character: sometimes distant, sometimes unstable, sometimes privately nostalgic.

Campaign idea

The visual system turns transition techniques into emotional storytelling. Each clip keeps the same hand-drawn house-and-robot world, but changes the way the audience enters it: through distorted vision, a blink, or a physical approach.

Selected final animations

Three ways into the same album world

01 · Vision Test

Blur to Clear

The first video uses repeated blur-to-focus cycles to mimic unstable sight. Longer blurred moments and shorter flashes of clarity make the scene feel tipsy and uncertain before a freeze-frame beat shifts the viewer into a computer-screen context.

  • controlled focus shift
  • freeze frame
  • nested screen transition

02 · Wake Up

Open / Close Eyes

The second video moves from the exterior house image into an interior bedroom moment. A blink becomes the trigger: the eye closure pauses the motion long enough for the transition to feel like a shift inward rather than a simple zoom.

  • camera push-in
  • blink trigger
  • memory-like replay

03 · Step Forward

Homebody Robot Walk

The third video treats the robot’s approach as emotional progression. Instead of enlarging the character through camera zoom, the robot walks forward, making intimacy feel gradual while the background gently sways around it.

  • scale through movement
  • secondary motion
  • performance timing

Design logic

Why the transitions work

01

Motivated triggers

The blink, freeze frame, and walking step are not decorative cuts. Each one gives the transition a reason to happen.

02

Readable emotion

The audience can track what changes and what remains consistent, so the emotional state is legible without over-explaining.

03

Consistent world

The same robot, house, pixel text, grassy green, sky blue, and pale paper texture connect the three separate song promotions.
